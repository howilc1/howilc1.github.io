If you’re renovating your kitchen or just want to upgrade your sink, installing a garbage disposal in a double sink is a great option. Not only can it make clean up easier, but it can also reduce unpleasant odors and help prevent clogs. However, if you’ve never installed a garbage disposal before, the process can seem daunting. Don’t worry, we’ve got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to install a garbage disposal in a double sink.

Pain Points

Many people are intimidated by the idea of installing a garbage disposal in a double sink. They worry it will be too complicated or that they don’t have the necessary skills. Others are concerned about the cost of hiring a professional to do the job. However, with a little bit of knowledge and the right tools, installing a garbage disposal is a relatively simple process that can save you money in the long run.

How to Install a Garbage Disposal in a Double Sink

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary tools and materials. These include a screwdriver, pliers, a putty knife, plumber’s putty, a garbage disposal, and a disposal installation kit. Once you have everything you need, follow these steps:

  1. Turn off the power to the sink by unplugging it from the wall or turning off the circuit breaker.
  2. Disconnect the sink’s drain pipes and remove the old sink flange.
  3. Install the new sink flange and apply plumber’s putty around the edges.
  4. Attach the mounting assembly to the sink flange and tighten the screws.
  5. Attach the garbage disposal to the mounting assembly, making sure it’s secure.
  6. Connect the drain pipes to the disposal and the sink.
  7. Turn on the power and test the garbage disposal.

If you’re unsure about any of the steps, consult the user manual that comes with your garbage disposal or watch a video tutorial online.

Summary of Installing a Garbage Disposal in a Double Sink

Installing a garbage disposal in a double sink may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and knowledge, it is a relatively simple process. The steps include turning off the power, disconnecting the sink’s drain pipes, installing the new sink flange, attaching the mounting assembly, affixing the garbage disposal, and connecting the drain pipes. The user manual that comes with your disposal or online tutorial videos can provide more detailed instructions.

Installing a Garbage Disposal: Personal Experience

However, I did some research and watched a few tutorial videos online, which gave me the confidence to tackle the job myself. I made sure to have all the necessary tools and materials on hand before I began, which made the process much smoother. Common Mistakes to Avoid

One of the most common mistakes people make when installing a garbage disposal is not using enough plumber’s putty around the sink flange. This can lead to leaks and other problems down the road. Another mistake is not tightening the mounting assembly enough, which can cause the disposal to loosen over time. Make sure to follow the instructions carefully and double-check your work as you go.

How to Maintain Your Garbage Disposal

Once your garbage disposal is installed, it’s important to take care of it to ensure it lasts as long as possible. Here are some tips for maintaining your disposal:

  • Always run cold water when using the disposal.
  • Avoid putting hard or fibrous materials down the disposal, such as bones or celery.
  • Regularly clean the disposal by grinding ice cubes and citrus peels.
  • If the disposal starts to smell bad, pour a cup of vinegar down it and let it sit for an hour before running water and turning on the disposal.

Question and Answer

1. What size garbage disposal do I need for a double sink?

You should choose a garbage disposal based on the size of your sink and your household’s usage. As a general rule, a 1/2 to 3/4 horsepower disposal is sufficient for most households with a double sink.

2. Can I install a garbage disposal myself, or should I hire a professional?

While it is possible to install a garbage disposal yourself, if you don’t have experience with plumbing or electrical work, you may want to consider hiring a professional. Professional installation ensures that the disposal is installed correctly and can help prevent future problems.

3. How much does it cost to install a garbage disposal in a double sink?

The cost of installing a garbage disposal in a double sink can vary depending on the type of disposal, the difficulty of the installation, and whether you choose to hire a professional. However, as a rough estimate, you can expect to pay anywhere from $200 to $500 for installation.

4. Is it safe to put my hand down the garbage disposal?

No, it is never safe to put your hand down the garbage disposal, even if it is turned off. Always use tongs or pliers to remove any objects that may be stuck inside.

Conclusion of How to Install a Garbage Disposal in a Double Sink

Installing a garbage disposal in a double sink is a relatively simple process that can save you time and hassle in the long run. By following the steps outlined in this guide and taking care of your disposal once it’s installed, you can enjoy a cleaner, more efficient kitchen in no time.
