Pain Points

One of the biggest pain points when it comes to installing sharkbite fittings on copper is the fear that it will leak or fail over time. Another concern is the cost, as sharkbite fittings tend to be more expensive than traditional copper fittings. Additionally, some people may be intimidated by the installation process and unsure of how to properly use the sharkbite fitting.

Answering the Target

Installing sharkbite fittings on copper is actually a lot easier than you may think. First, make sure the copper pipe is cut cleanly and free of any burrs or rough edges. Then, simply slide the sharkbite fitting onto the pipe and ensure it is fully seated. No need for any soldering or special tools!

Summary of Main Points

In summary, installing sharkbite fittings on copper is a simple and cost-effective solution for those looking to avoid traditional copper fitting methods. By following a few easy steps, you can ensure a successful installation and have peace of mind knowing your fittings will not fail in the future.

How to Install Sharkbite Fittings on Copper: Step-by-Step Guide

At my plumbing company, we’ve found that sharkbite fittings are a popular choice among customers. They’re easy to install and we’ve had great success with them, which is why I wanted to share my personal experience with you on how to install sharkbite fittings on copper.

First, make sure you have all the necessary tools and materials on hand, including sharkbite fittings, a pipe cutter, and a deburring tool. Cut the copper piping cleanly and make sure there are no rough edges or burrs. Then, slide the sharkbite fitting onto the pipe and ensure it is fully seated. You’ll hear a satisfying “click” sound when it’s properly installed. That’s it! No need for any soldering or special tools.

It’s important to note that sharkbite fittings can only be used with copper pipes, so if you have another type of piping in your home, this method may not work for you. Additionally, sharkbite fittings should not be used in areas that are exposed to direct sunlight as UV rays can break down the fittings over time.

Tips for a Successful Installation

One tip for a successful installation is to make sure you cut the copper piping cleanly and use a deburring tool to remove any rough edges. This will ensure a snug fit and prevent any leaks. Additionally, be sure to apply pressure to the sharkbite fitting when installing to ensure it’s fully seated.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

One common mistake when installing sharkbite fittings on copper is not cutting the pipe cleanly or removing burrs, which can lead to leaks in the future. Another mistake is not applying enough pressure to the fitting when installing, which can also cause leaks. Finally, be sure to follow the manufacturer’s instructions carefully and don’t assume you know how to install the fitting without reading the instructions first.

Additional Information

Sharkbite fittings are a great solution for those looking to avoid traditional copper fitting methods.
