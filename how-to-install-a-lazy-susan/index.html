If you’re looking to increase the functionality of your kitchen cabinets, installing a lazy susan is a great option that not only saves space but also makes finding items in your cabinets much easier. But the process of installing a lazy susan can be overwhelming to many homeowners. Don’t worry, we’ve got you covered! In this article, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know about how to install a lazy susan in your kitchen cabinets.

The Pain Points of Installing a Lazy Susan

Many homeowners run into trouble when trying to install a lazy susan due to its seemingly complicated design. The installation process requires proper measuring, planning, and use of specialized tools, which can make the process seem daunting. Not to mention, if you make a mistake, it can be challenging to fix without causing more damage to the cabinet. That’s why it’s essential to approach the installation process with a clear plan and take the proper steps to ensure everything goes smoothly.

The Target of How to Install a Lazy Susan

The process of installing a lazy susan requires four main steps: measuring, planning, installing the brackets, and attaching the lazy susan hardware. Once you understand these steps and have the proper tools, the process becomes much more straightforward.

Main Points to Remember:

Measure the cabinet to ensure proper fit, plan the placement and placement of brackets, and attach lazy susan hardware. Lastly, test the lazy susan to make sure it spins smoothly with no issues.

Installation Step by Step

First, measure the inside of the kitchen cabinet where you plan to install the lazy susan. Be sure to measure the depth, height, and width. Then, plan where you want to install the bracket and the lazy susan hardware.

Next, install the brackets in your desired location by marking and drilling holes that allow for the brackets to be securely installed. Typically, brackets will line the full height of the cabinet side panels, one near the top and one near the bottom of the cabinet. Follow the manufacturer’s instructions to ensure proper installation.

Finally, attach the lazy susan hardware to the brackets. The hardware will include a top pivot, base pivot, and a set of ball bearings that act as a gliding mechanism that allows the lazy susan to rotate smoothly.

Additional Tips for Installing a Lazy Susan

Before you start the installation process, make sure you have all the necessary tools and materials, including a drill, drill bits, measuring tape, brackets, screws, and the lazy susan hardware itself. Double-check all measurements and marks to ensure everything is lined up correctly before drilling into the cabinet. Finally, test the lazy susan to see if it spins smoothly.

Maintenance and Care of a Lazy Susan

Once installed, a lazy susan requires minimal maintenance. Make sure to sweep up any crumbs or debris that may accumulate over time. The ball bearings used in the mechanism should stay lubricated for the lazy susan to glide with ease. You can use a small amount of lubricant to maintain the mechanism and prevent any rust

Personal Experience with Installing a Lazy Susan

When I first decided to install a lazy susan in my kitchen, I was a bit intimidated by the process. But after taking the proper measurements and planning out the installation, the process was much more manageable than I initially thought. The key was having the right tools and following the manufacturer’s instructions carefully. Now the lazy susan has become an essential part of my kitchen organization and makes finding items in my cabinets a breeze.

Common Questions about Installing a Lazy Susan

Q: What is the best way to measure for a lazy susan?

A: Measure the inside depth, height, and width of your kitchen cabinet. Then, consider the size of your lazy susan and add an additional 1 to 2 inches clearance on all sides to ensure it fits comfortably.

Q: Can I install a lazy susan in an existing cabinet?

A: Yes, you can install a lazy susan in an existing cabinet. You will need to make sure the dimensions of your existing cabinet are suitable for the lazy susan you’re installing, and the installation process may be challenging depending on how the cabinet was built.

Q: How many brackets do I need to install a lazy susan?

A: Typically, for a standard-sized lazy susan, two brackets are needed to ensure proper support.

Q: What is the average time it takes to install a lazy susan?

A: Depending on your experience level, the size and condition of your existing cabinet, and the size of your lazy susan, the installation process can take anywhere from one hour to a full day to complete.

Conclusion of How to Install a Lazy Susan

Installing a lazy susan in your kitchen cabinets is an excellent way to increase storage space and improve organization. While the installation process may seem daunting at first, with proper planning and the right tools, you can install a lazy susan with ease. Remember to measure carefully, plan the installation location, install brackets carefully, and attach the lazy susan hardware for a smooth operation.
