If you’re updating your bathroom, you might want to consider installing a pop up drain. Not only does it give your sink a sleek and modern look, it also makes cleaning up easier. In this post, we’ll walk you through how to install a pop up drain, step by step.

Pain Points

In today’s fast-paced world, we’re always looking for ways to save time and make our lives easier. Installing a pop up drain can do just that. Have you ever struggled to clean your sink because of a drain that doesn’t pop up? Or have you lost jewelry or small items down the drain? These are all pain points that can be solved by installing a pop up drain.

How to Install a Pop Up Drain

Before you get started, make sure you have all the necessary tools such as a wrench, plumber’s putty, and Teflon tape. Begin by removing the old drain, cleaning the sink and drain hole, and then applying plumber’s putty around the new drain. Insert the drain into the sink and tighten it with a wrench. Attach the pivot rod to the drain and connect it to the stopper. Finally, connect the pop up lever and overflow to the drain and adjust it as needed.

Summary

In summary, installing a pop up drain can solve common pain points such as difficulty cleaning and losing small items down the drain. Follow these steps: remove the old drain, clean the area, apply plumber’s putty, insert the new drain, attach the pivot rod to the stopper, connect the pop up lever and overflow, and adjust as needed. Now, let’s dive a bit deeper into each step.

Step 1: Removing the Old Drain

First, remove the old drain by loosening the nut underneath the sink and pulling the drain out. Be sure to have a bucket handy to catch any water that may come out. Clean the area around the drain hole and the sink itself with a mild detergent and water.

Step 2: Applying Plumber’s Putty

Now it’s time to apply the plumber’s putty to the new drain. Take a small amount of the putty and roll it into a rope shape. Place the putty around the flange on the underside of the new drain. Insert the drain into the sink and press down firmly to create a seal. Wipe away any excess putty with a wet rag.

Step 3: Inserting the New Drain

Once you have applied the plumber’s putty, insert the new drain into the sink. From underneath the sink, thread the nut onto the drain and tighten it with a wrench. Be sure not to overtighten as this can cause damage to the sink and the drainage system.

Step 4: Attaching the Pivot Rod and Stopper

Next, attach the pivot rod to the drain and connect it to the stopper. The pivot rod is what controls the opening and closing of the stopper. Place the stopper in the drain hole and insert the pivot rod through the back of the faucet. Tighten the pivot rod nut to secure it in place.

Step 5: Connecting the Pop Up Lever and Overflow

Finally, connect the pop up lever and overflow to the drain. The pop up lever is what controls the opening and closing of the drain while the overflow prevents water from overflowing. Adjust the linkage as needed and test to make sure everything is working properly.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I install a pop up drain myself or do I need to hire a professional?
A: While it’s possible to install a pop up drain yourself, it’s important to have the necessary tools and knowledge. If you’re not confident in your abilities, it’s best to leave it to a professional.

Q: How long does it take to install a pop up drain?
A: Installation time can vary depending on your level of experience and the complexity of the job. On average, it can take anywhere from 1-2 hours.

Q: Do I need to purchase any special tools or materials?
A: Yes, you’ll need to have a wrench, plumber’s putty, and Teflon tape on hand. It’s also a good idea to have a bucket or large container to catch any water that may come out during the installation process.

Q: Can I install a pop up drain on any sink?
A: Most sinks are compatible with pop up drains. However, it’s important to make sure that the new drain and the sink itself are compatible before beginning the installation process.

Conclusion

Installing a pop up drain in your bathroom sink can save you time and hassle in the long run. By following these steps and having the necessary tools on hand, you can give your sink a modern and sleek upgrade. Remember, if you’re not comfortable with the installation process, it’s always best to hire a professional.
