Answer

To install blinds outside mount, you’ll need a tape measure, a drill, a screwdriver, and screws. Start by measuring the width and height of the area where you want to install the blinds. It’s essential to align the brackets correctly and ensure that the blinds are level before drilling in the screws. Mistakes to Avoid

When installing blinds outside mount, there are a few mistakes you should avoid. One of the most common errors is making improper measurements. If the blinds are too long or too short, they won’t function correctly. Additionally, not aligning the brackets correctly can cause unsightly gaps, reducing the amount of privacy and light control you receive. Finally, not checking that the blinds open and close smoothly can cause unnecessary wear and tear on your new blinds.

Tips

To avoid the pitfalls of incorrect measurements or faulty bracket alignment, double-check your work before drilling in the screws.
