Sunpass mini is one of the smallest transponders that makes toll payments easy for drivers on Florida’s toll roads. Installing this mini device on the windshield can be a little tricky, but it takes less than a minute if you know the correct steps. This article will guide you on how to install sunpass mini, so you don’t have to worry about paying cash or getting stuck in a toll booth line. Pain Points Related to Installing Sunpass Mini

Installing Sunpass mini can be challenging if you are unaware of the correct steps. Sunpass Mini is a small device that can be easily lost or misplaced during installation. Incorrect installation can cause obstruction and affect the device’s accuracy in toll payment. Therefore, it is necessary to understand the correct installation process and avoid any inconvenience.

How to Install Sunpass Mini

The first step to install Sunpass mini is to clean the inside of the windshield where you plan to place the device. Make sure the area is free from any dust, oil, or grime. Then, remove the Sunpass mini from the package and remove the adhesive strip from the back.

Hold the Sunpass mini face up and position it on the cleaned part of the windshield. Press firmly on the device for 10-20 seconds to make sure it is secure. After installation, wait for at least one hour before use. This allows the adhesive to settle and ensure the device’s proper functioning.

Summary of How to Install Sunpass Mini

To sum up, installing Sunpass mini takes only a few easy steps. Cleaning the windshield, removing the adhesive strip, placing the Sunpass mini on the windshield, and pressing it firmly are simple steps that will help you avoid long toll booth lines. Remember to wait for an hour before using Sunpass mini to allow the adhesive to settle and ensure proper functioning. By following these simple steps, you can easily install Sunpass mini.

Positioning Sunpass Mini

While installing Sunpass mini, the location of the transponder on the windshield plays a vital role. Avoid obstructing the eyesight of the driver while placing the device. It’s recommended to install Sunpass mini on the upper left-hand side or right-hand side of the windshield, behind the rearview mirror. This placement ensures that Sunpass mini does not obstruct the driver’s view while driving.

Removing Sunpass Mini

To remove Sunpass mini, take a firm grip on the device and try to lift the edges. If you used a velcro attachment, you could use your fingers to release the Velcro, and the device will come off. For adhesive attachments, use a plastic card or scraper like an old bank card to pry the device from the windshield gently.

### Problems While Installing Sunpass Mini

Question and Answer about How to Install Sunpass Mini

Q. What is Sunpass Mini?

A. Sunpass mini is a small, pocket-sized transponder that makes toll payments easy for drivers on Florida’s toll roads.

Q. What is the cost of Sunpass Mini?

A. Sunpass mini cost $4.99, and it comes preloaded with $5.00 of toll credit.

Q. Can I use Sunpass Mini out-of-state?

A. Yes, you can use Sunpass mini on all toll roads in Florida, Georgia, and North Carolina. Sunpass mini is interoperable with other states’ toll-collection systems, including E-ZPass, but you need to register your Sunpass mini for out-of-state operation.

Q. Can Sunpass Mini be used in rental cars?

A. Yes, you can use Sunpass mini in rental cars by attaching it to the windshield using the velcro attachment provided. However, rental car companies may also provide their toll-collection systems, so it’s best to check with your rental car company beforehand.
