Are you looking to upgrade your flooring but worried about the cost and effort it takes to remove and replace your existing floors? Fear not, because installing a subfloor over your existing floor can save you time and money while providing a sturdy and level base for your new flooring. Read on to learn more about how to install subfloor over existing floor.

Pain Points of Installing Subfloor Over Existing Floor

Removing and disposing of your existing flooring can be a time-consuming and expensive process. Additionally, if your existing floor is level and undamaged, it can seem wasteful to tear it up for the sake of a new subfloor. Installing a subfloor over your existing floor allows you to save time and money while still providing a sturdy and level base for your new flooring.

How to Install Subfloor Over Existing Floor

First, ensure that your existing floor is clean and level. Then, lay down a layer of subfloor adhesive before attaching tongue and groove plywood panels to your existing floor with screws. Be sure to stagger the seams of your panels and leave a small gap between each panel to account for expansion. Finally, sand down any high spots and fill in any gaps with wood filler before laying down your new flooring.

Summary of Main Points

Installing a subfloor over your existing floor can save you time and money while still providing a sturdy and level base for your new flooring. To install a subfloor, clean and level your existing floor, lay down subfloor adhesive, attach tongue and groove plywood panels, and sand and fill before installing your new flooring.

How to Lay Down Tongue and Groove Plywood Panels

When I installed a subfloor over my existing floor, I opted for tongue and groove plywood panels for added stability. First, I measured my room to determine how many panels I would need, and then I cut each panel to size. I then laid down a layer of subfloor adhesive and pressed the first panel into place. From there, I attached the panel to the existing floor with screws before inserting the tongue of the next panel into the groove of the first panel. I continued this process until the entire floor was covered in plywood panels.

How to Cover Expansion Gaps

When laying down the tongue and groove plywood panels, it’s important to leave a small gap between each panel to account for expansion. I covered these gaps by sanding down any high spots on the panels and then filling in the gaps with wood filler. Once the filler dried, I sanded it down to create a smooth and level surface for my new flooring.

Securing the Subfloor to the Existing Floor

When attaching the tongue and groove plywood panels to the existing floor, be sure to use screws to secure them in place. This will ensure that your subfloor is sturdy and level, providing a solid base for your new flooring.

Question and Answer

Q: Can any type of flooring be installed over a subfloor?

A: Yes, a wide variety of flooring types can be installed over a subfloor, including hardwood, vinyl, and tile.

Q: Do I need to remove my existing baseboards before installing a subfloor?

A: It is not necessary to remove your existing baseboards before installing a subfloor, but you may need to replace them or add quarter round molding to cover the expansion gap left between the subfloor and the wall.

Q: Will installing a subfloor over my existing floor make my ceiling lower?

A: Installing a subfloor over your existing floor will not affect your ceiling height, as the added height is minimal and will not be noticeable.

Q: How long does it take to install a subfloor over an existing floor?

A: The time it takes to install a subfloor over an existing floor will depend on the size of the room and the complexity of the job. In general, it can take anywhere from a few days to a week or more to complete.

Conclusion of How to Install Subfloor Over Existing Floor

Installing a subfloor over your existing floor can save you time and money while still providing a sturdy and level base for your new flooring. By following these steps and tips, you can install a subfloor with ease and confidence, ensuring a successful and cost-effective flooring upgrade.
